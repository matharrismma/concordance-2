# Calibre 44 — Case & Bracelet System

## External Design Specification — Rev A

**Date:** April 7, 2026
**Companion to:** Calibre 44 Movement Spec Rev J (Locked)

---

## 1. Design Intent

One watch, two personalities. On the integrated bracelet, it reads as a confident, angular, sports-luxury piece in the tradition of the great integrated-bracelet watches. On a leather or sailcloth strap, the bracelet's geometry disappears and the watch reveals its true case shape: round, refined, classical — a proper dress watch.

The bracelet creates the angularity. The case holds the restraint.

---

## 2. Case Architecture

### 2.1 Core Shape

The case is fundamentally **round.** Not cushion, not tonneau, not octagonal. A true circle in plan view with a thin, sharply defined bezel edge. The sides are gently curved — not slab-sided — tapering slightly from bezel to caseback. This gives the profile a slim, refined appearance on strap.

The bezel is **not a separate part.** It is machined as an integral raised edge of the unibody case top (which is also the dial surface). The bezel chamfer is the single polished accent on an otherwise brushed case. This is where the light catches.

### 2.3 Case Band Profile

The case band transitions through three zones from bezel to caseback:

**Top zone (0–3 mm from bezel):** Vertical or near-vertical wall. Brushed horizontal. This is the zone visible when looking at the watch face-on. Clean, flat, disciplined.

**Mid zone (3–7 mm):** Gentle inward curve. Brushed vertically (direction change creates a subtle visual break). This curvature is what makes the watch feel thin on the wrist despite the 11mm spec. It tucks under toward the skin side.

**Lower zone (7–11 mm):** Curves back out slightly to meet the caseback ring. This creates a comfortable wrist contact surface and gives room for the caseback thread.

## 3. Hidden Lug System

### 3.1 Concept

At 12 o'clock and 6 o'clock, the case band extends rearward (toward the caseback) by approximately **3 mm** beyond the main case circle. These extensions are not visible from the front — they sit below the bezel plane and behind the case band curvature. From directly above, the watch reads as a pure circle.

The underside of each extension contains a **recessed channel, 22mm wide and 2mm deep,** running perpendicular to the 12–6 axis. This channel accepts either:
- The bracelet end-link (slides in from the side and locks)
- A standard curved spring bar for strap attachment

### 3.2 Channel Geometry

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Channel width | 22.0 mm |
| Channel depth | 2.0 mm |
| Channel height (front to back) | 3.0 mm |
| Spring bar bore diameter | 1.2 mm |
| Spring bar bore depth | 1.5 mm (each side) |
| Spring bar diameter | 1.0 mm (quick-release type) |
| Channel surface | Brushed, radiused edges |

The spring bar bores are drilled into the inner side walls of the channel, hidden from view. They accept standard 22mm quick-release spring bars. When a strap is installed, the strap end tucks into the channel and curves down toward the wrist. The channel walls frame the strap end cleanly — no exposed spring bar, no visible mechanism.

### 3.3 End-Link Interface

The bracelet end-links use the same spring bar bores but add a secondary retention feature: a small detent pin on the end-link body that clicks into a dimple machined into the channel wall. This provides positive rotational location so the end-link sits precisely aligned with the case contour. The detent is overcome by the quick-release lever — pull the tab, slide the end-link out sideways.

### 3.4 Appearance — Bracelet Attached

When the end-links are installed, they fill the channel completely and extend the case profile outward. The end-link top surface is flush with the case band bottom edge. The transition from case to end-link to bracelet is seamless — one continuous form. The lug-to-lug reads 48mm. The watch appears to have integrated lugs that are part of the case, though they are actually part of the bracelet.

### 3.5 Appearance — Strap Attached

When the end-links are removed and a strap is installed, the case extensions are barely visible — they present as a subtle 3mm shelf at 12 and 6, gently curved to match the case band profile. The strap emerges from under this shelf and curves to the wrist. The lug-to-lug drops to 44mm. The watch reads as a compact round dress watch on a strap. The hidden channel is not visible from any normal viewing angle.

### 3.6 Strap Change Procedure

1. Turn watch over
2. Locate quick-release tab on spring bar (visible in channel)
3. Slide tab with fingernail or tool tip
4. End-link or strap releases from one side
5. Repeat other side
6. Slide new end-link or strap into channel
7. Spring bar clicks into bore

No tools required for strap changes. End-link changes may benefit from a small tool to engage the detent, but fingernail pressure is sufficient.

## 4. Bracelet Design

### 4.1 Character

The bracelet is where the Royal Oak lives. Flat, faceted, architectural links with alternating brushed and polished surfaces. The bracelet adds angularity and presence that the round case alone does not have. When attached, it transforms the watch from classical to contemporary.

### 4.2 Link Architecture

Three-piece link construction: a wide center link flanked by two narrower outer links. This gives visual rhythm and allows the bracelet to articulate around the wrist with a smooth, weighted drape.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Bracelet width at end-link | 22 mm |
| Bracelet width at clasp | 18 mm (gentle taper) |
| Center link width | 12 mm |
| Outer link width | 5 mm (each side) |
| Link thickness | 3.5 mm |
| Link length (12–6 direction) | 7 mm |
| Total bracelet length | ~180 mm (adjustable with removable links) |
| Material | 304 SS |

### 4.3 Link Surfaces

**Center link:** Flat top surface, **brushed horizontal** (same direction as case band top zone). The flat plane catches light differently than the curved case, creating contrast. Edges are chamfered at 45° and **polished** — thin bright lines running the length of each link.

**Outer links:** Flat top surface, **polished.** This creates the alternating brushed/polished rhythm visible when looking at the bracelet from above. The polish picks up the same polished chamfer language from the bezel edge.

**Underside (all links):** Brushed, gently curved for wrist comfort. No sharp edges.

### 4.4 End-Links

The end-links are the key transitional part. Each end-link is a single sculpted piece that:

- Slides into the hidden channel on the case underside
- Presents a **faceted, angular exterior** that visually transforms the case geometry
- Matches the bracelet width and link rhythm exactly
- Has a brushed flat top surface with polished chamfered edges (matching center links)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Length (extending from case) | 6 mm |
| Width | 22 mm |
| Thickness | Matches case band contour (~4 mm tapering) |
| Material | 304 SS (same stock as case) |
| Retention | Quick-release spring bar + detent pin |

The end-link exterior profile has **two flat facets** angled at approximately 15° from horizontal, creating a shallow V-shape when viewed from the side. This angularity is what gives the "integrated lug" appearance. The facets are brushed, with polished edges between them.

### 4.5 Clasp

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Type | Butterfly deployant, dual push-button release |
| Width | 18 mm |
| Material | 304 SS |
| Finish | Brushed body, polished buttons |
| Logo | Engraved on exterior plate |
| Security | Double-lock — buttons must be pressed simultaneously |
| Micro-adjust | 3 positions, ~2 mm per step (6 mm total range) |
| Extension | Optional single-fold diver's extension (+20 mm) |

The clasp is flush with the bracelet profile when closed. No proud edges, no exposed hinges. The push buttons sit in machined recesses.

## 10. Manufacturing Notes

### 10.1 Case (Signal Industrial Solutions)

The unibody case with hidden lug channels is a 5-axis CNC operation. The channels are the most complex feature — they require undercut machining from the caseback side. Sequence:

1. Rough mill exterior profile from billet (3-axis)
2. Rough mill interior movement cavity (3-axis)
3. Machine lug extension profiles at 12 and 6 (5-axis)
4. Machine hidden channels from underside (5-axis, undercut)
5. Drill spring bar bores and detent dimples (5-axis)
6. Finish mill bezel, case band, crown recess (5-axis)
7. Precision bore all movement features per Calibre 44 spec
8. Tap, deburr, finish

### 10.2 Bracelet Links

Center and outer links are individually CNC-milled from 304 SS bar. Pin holes drilled on fixture. Links assembled on stainless steel pins with screw-in retention (removable for sizing). Each link is finish-brushed or polished per spec before assembly.

### 10.3 End-Links

The most complex bracelet component. Each end-link requires 5-axis milling to achieve the faceted exterior, the channel interface geometry, and the spring bar / detent features. Made from same 304 SS stock as the case for perfect color match.
