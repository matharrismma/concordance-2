## 1. What This Watch Is

A high-quality American-made automatic wristwatch, designed to be purchased once and passed down. Not a fashion piece. Not a statement of exclusivity. A precision instrument built with modern CNC and laser manufacturing, finished by the machine, honest about what it is.

The owner wears it five days a week. It keeps good time. It looks right with work clothes or a suit. When it needs service in ten years, any competent watch shop in any city can open it, understand it, service it with standard tools and catalog parts, and hand it back running. And then again in another ten years. And again after that.

### 1.1 Design Principles

**Harrison's constancy.** Flat torque delivery from a generously sized barrel. The escapement sees consistent energy whether the spring is at full wind or two days in. Stable amplitude means stable rate.

**Railroad ruggedness.** Oversized pivots. Thick gear teeth. Generous clearances. The watch is overbuilt for daily life — not because the engineering demands it, but because the owner's life does.

**Any shop can fix it.** No proprietary tools. No single-source materials. No silicon. No components that require a factory return. Standard catalog jewels in serviceable bronze bushings. A standard 3 Hz Swiss lever escapement with global parts availability. When something wears out, it gets replaced — by whoever the owner trusts to do the work.

**Machine-honest finish.** CNC and laser ARE the finishing process. Brushed steel, clean geometry, precise lines. The exhibition caseback shows engineering, not decoration. There are no Geneva stripes pretending to be hand-applied. The beauty is the accuracy of the machine work itself.

### 2.1 Why 3 Hz

The 3 Hz Swiss lever is the most proven, most widely sourced, and most globally serviceable escapement architecture in the world. Balance assemblies, pallet forks, escape wheels, and shock systems are commodity items available from multiple suppliers on every continent. Any watchmaker who has serviced an ETA 2824 or Sellita SW200 understands this escapement.

At 3 Hz, positional stability is strong enough to approach chronometer performance with good regulation: ≤8 seconds across five positions, ±5 s/d regulated. This is better than most watches at any price point.

### 2.2 Why No Manual Winding

The watch is designed for daily wear. The rotor charges the barrel. The crown does one thing: set the time. This eliminates the winding pinion, clutch, and a second crown position — three fewer parts, one fewer mechanism to fail, one fewer thing to explain to the owner.

### 2.3 Why No Seconds Hand

Eliminating the seconds display removes the need for a seconds pinion extension and the hacking complication (stop lever, spring, contact geometry). Hours and minutes — like the original marine chronometers.

## 3. The Unibody

### 3.1 Concept

The watch case IS the mainplate. The dial IS the case top. There is no separate case body, no mainplate, no movement ring, no dial disc, no dial feet. One block of 304 stainless steel, CNC-milled from billet, contains all pivot bores (via serviceable bronze bushings), barrel pocket, rotor bearing seat, crystal seat, caseback thread, dial surface, and bridge attachment points.

### 3.2 Space Advantage

| Architecture | Case OD | Wall | Ring | Plate Edge | Usable |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conventional | 42 mm | 2 mm | 1 mm | 1 mm | 34 mm |
| Unibody | 42 mm | 2 mm | — | — | 38 mm |
| Equivalent conventional | 46 mm | 2 mm | 1 mm | 1 mm | 38 mm |

### 3.3 The Integrated Dial

The exterior top face of the unibody IS the dial. After all interior machining is complete, the top surface is sunburst-brushed from center using CNC or laser. Applied polished steel indices are pressed into pre-drilled holes at the cardinal positions. Remaining hour markers are laser-engraved.

A precision bore at center allows the cannon pinion and hand arbors to pass through. The motion works sit in a shallow recess machined into the underside of the dial surface, retained by a thin dial-side plate secured with two screws.

### 3.5 Assembly Sequence

Everything installs from the caseback side:

1. Unibody sits dial-face down in Provident Tool fixture (42mm jaws)
2. Press bronze bushings into all pivot and jewel bore recesses
3. Press jewels into bushings
4. Install barrel module into pocket
5. Install gear train (center, third, fourth, escape)
6. Install escapement and balance assembly
7. Install keyless works
8. Seat monobloc bridge, secure with screws
9. Install ratchet wheel on barrel arbor
10. Install rotor bearing and rotor
11. Flip case, install hands through dial center bore
12. Seat crystal with gasket
13. Thread caseback with gasket
14. Install crown and stem

## 4. Serviceable Bronze Bushings

### 4.1 Purpose

Every pivot bore and jewel seat in the unibody uses a removable phosphor bronze bushing. The unibody contains rough-tolerance cylindrical recesses. The bushings are precision-bored to final jewel and pivot dimensions. If a bushing is damaged or worn, a watchmaker drives it out with a standard staking tool and presses in a replacement. The unibody itself never needs re-machining.

This is what makes the unibody concept honest for a 50-year watch. Without bushings, a damaged jewel seat in the unibody would be catastrophic — the entire case would need replacement. With bushings, it is a routine service operation.

### 5.1 Layout

| Position | Wheel | Pinion | Ratio | Pitch Diameter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Barrel | 72T | — | — | 12.96 mm |
| Center | 64T | 9T | 8:1 | W 11.52, P 1.62 mm |
| Third | 64T | 8T | 8:1 | W 11.52, P 1.44 mm |
| Fourth | 64T | 8T | 8:1 | W 11.52, P 1.44 mm |
| Escape | 15T | 8T | 8:1 | W 2.70, P 1.44 mm |

**Total ratio:** 8 × 8 × 8 × 8 = 4,096:1

**Power reserve:** 10 mainspring turns × 4,096 × 2 × 15 ÷ 21,600 = **56.9 hours ≈ 57 hours**

Every mesh sees the same 8:1 load ratio. Same engagement geometry. Same wear pattern.

### 5.2 Manufacturing Advantage

Third and fourth wheel+pinion are **identical parts.** Three unique wheel+pinion geometries in the train. Five total gear-cutting setups for the entire movement.

## 19. Service Model

### 18.1 Standard 10-Year Service

Complete disassembly. Ultrasonic cleaning. Bushing inspection — replace if worn. Jewel inspection — replace if chipped. Fresh lubrication. New gaskets. Timing regulation. Water resistance test.

### 18.2 Parts Availability

Provident Precision maintains inventory of all bushing families, jewel sizes, mainspring spec, gasket sizes, and escapement kit components. A watch shop orders a service kit and receives everything for a standard service in one package.

### 18.3 No Factory Return

Every part is a catalog item or simple machined piece. Any watchmaker with a staking set, timing machine, and the 20-page service manual can do the work.

### 18.4 Service Kit Contents

- Bushing set (14 pcs, pre-loaded with jewels)
- Mainspring
- Gasket set (crown O-rings, caseback, crystal)
- Lubricant applicator set
- Service manual (if first service)
