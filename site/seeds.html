Seeds of the Word

Paul, grieved by the idols of Athens, still found an altar to an unknown god — and said, "What you worship in ignorance, this I proclaim to you" (Acts 17:23). He mined the true fragment even from pagan poets, named its source, turned it against the idol, and pointed it to Christ. This does the same: found and attributed, never generated; each fragment is CONCORDANT — a signpost, never a proof, never sealed as math. The idol is named and refused; the fragment points to the Gate — it is never the Gate.

Mining, not blending. The seed is kept, the error rejected, and the Source is named — Jesus Christ, "in whom all things hold together" (Colossians 1:17; 1 John 4:1-3). A signpost is not a seal — see what we prove, and what we don't.
